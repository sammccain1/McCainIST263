Man Coverage Overview

The coverages that will be shown on this page include Cover 0 and Cover 1

Cover 0

Techniques

Man to man coverage across the board. Corners responsible for #1 receiver, Apex Defenders are responsible for #2, and Mike is responsible for #3. "Banjo" check vs bunch formations, corner takes 1st outside route and apex takes first inside route.

Strengths

Great coverage in short yardage situations and in the red zone. Exploits 5 man protections in empty formations. Great coverage for generating pressure on the quarterback.

Weaknesses

Vulnerable against deep routes with no safety help for any defender. Requires corners to be excellent man to man defenders. Talented receivers who thrive in 1 on 1 matchups are more likely to break off big plays vs Cover 0.

Cover 1

Techniques

Man to man coverage across the board. Corners responsible for #1 receiver, Apex Defenders are responsible for #2, and Mike is responsible for #3. One safety will play the deep middle of the field looking for post routes from #1 or go routes from #2. Another safety will play the robber and look for crossing routes over the middle of the field. "Banjo" check vs bunch formations, corner takes 1st outside route and apex takes first inside route.

Strengths

Strong coverage against weak receiving cores, and can be used with many blitzes. Can be used against the run by committing extra bodies in the box. Is commonly used on 1st down and 2nd + 3rd & long.

Weaknesses

Similar to Cover 0, can be exploited by talented receivers for big plays, especially in the Red Zone and on short downs. Rub routes can exploit man defenders navigating through traffic on crossing routes. It is important to play in "limbo" alignment. Motions can make man to man coverage very difficult to disguise.
